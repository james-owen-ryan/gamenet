Explore a network of nearly 12,000 games that are linked to one another according to how related they are.

GameNet is a tool for game discovery created at the UC Santa Cruz Center for Games and Playable Media, as part of the larger Game Metadata and Citation Project (GAMECIP). Its development was lead by graduate students James Ryan and Eric Kaltman, with professors Noah Wardrip-Fruin and Michael Mateas as their primary advisers. It belongs to a suite of related tools that can all be found here.

For technical details about how GameNet works and other information, see our FAQ, or feel free to contact us.
